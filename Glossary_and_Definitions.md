# Glossary & Definitions  
Tesla × Quantara — Engineering Document

---

## Purpose

This glossary provides clear, standardized definitions for all technical terms, subsystem references, test procedures, and engineering concepts used across the Tesla × Quantara documentation set.  
It ensures consistent understanding across engineering, manufacturing, software, robotics, aerospace, and compliance teams.

---

## A

**Actuator**  
A mechanical device that produces movement, torque, or force.

**Ambient Temperature Range**  
Expected external temperature in which a subsystem must safely operate.

**Assembly Tolerance**  
Permissible variation in dimensions during component assembly.

---

## B

**Baseline Drift**  
Slow deviation of thermal, electrical, or mechanical values from their initial stable state.

**Bonding Interface**  
Surface where two materials or components are joined.

**Bus Protocol**  
Communication standard used to exchange data between subsystems.

---

## C

**Calibration**  
Process of adjusting sensors or controls to ensure measurement accuracy.

**Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE)**  
Rate at which a material expands when heated.

**Control Loop**  
Software system that monitors state and adjusts subsystem behavior in real time.

**Cooling Curve**  
Measured temperature decrease over time during cooldown.

---

## D

**Data Integrity**  
Accuracy and reliability of transmitted data.

**Diagnostic Log**  
Record of subsystem events, measurements, and warnings.

**Drift Compensation**  
Software algorithm correcting gradual value deviations.

---

## E

**Electrical Noise**  
Unwanted variations in voltage/current affecting stability or sensors.

**Emergency Override**  
System function that initiates immediate safety action when thresholds are exceeded.

**End-of-Line (EOL) Test**  
Final verification performed after manufacturing.

---

## F

**Fault Injection Test**  
Induced failure to verify safe fallback behavior.

**Fatigue**  
Material weakening under repeated stress cycles.

**Fit Check**  
Physical compatibility evaluation during subsystem integration.

---

## G

**Ground Path**  
Electrical pathway ensuring safe discharge of excess current.

---

## H

**Heartbeat Signal**  
Regular signal verifying subsystem operational health.

**Heatmap**  
Visual map showing temperature distribution across a component or subsystem.

---

## I

**Interface Specification**  
Document defining physical, electrical, data, and software connections.

**Insulation Breakdown Voltage**  
Voltage at which electrical insulation fails.

**Integration Test**  
Verification that combined components work as expected.

---

## L

**Load Profile**  
Expected pattern of electrical or mechanical load over time.

**Long-Duration Operation**  
Extended operation without shutdown, typically 24–72 hours or more.

---

## M

**Mechanical Stress**  
Forces acting on a component such as tension, compression, or torsion.

**Mitigation Strategy**  
Plan to reduce the likelihood or impact of identified risks.

---

## N

**Nominal Operating State**  
Normal, expected subsystem condition during operation.

---

## O

**Overshoot**  
When electrical or thermal values exceed their target range.

---

## P

**Predictive Model**  
Algorithm estimating future subsystem behavior based on current data.

**Process Capability (Cpk)**  
Statistical measure showing how consistently a process stays within tolerances.

---

## Q

**Quality Assurance (QA)**  
Processes ensuring manufacturing consistency and reliability.

---

## R

**Redundancy Path**  
Backup channel ensuring continued operation during failure.

**Resonance Zone**  
Vibration frequency range causing increased amplitude.

---

## S

**Safe State**  
Reduced-risk condition activated during faults or instability.

**Sensor Dropout**  
Interruption in sensor data delivery.

**Subsystem Synchronization**  
Coordination of timing and behavior across multiple subsystems.

---

## T

**Thermal Runaway**  
Uncontrolled rise in temperature once feedback cycles exceed safe limits.

**Torque Limit**  
Maximum allowable twisting force on mechanical components.

---

## U

**Unit Test**  
Validation of a single software module.

---

## V

**Voltage Stability**  
Consistency of voltage delivered during varying load.

**Vibration Profile**  
Measured vibration characteristics of a subsystem.

---

## W

**Wear Rate**  
Speed at which mechanical components degrade over time.

---

## Summary

This glossary provides standardized, universal terminology across the Tesla × Quantara engineering documentation.  
It is designed to ensure clarity, consistency, and alignment across all teams and integration efforts.
